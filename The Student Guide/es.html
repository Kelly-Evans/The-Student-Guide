How to Deal With Exam Stress

The Student Guide's advice for surviving the exam period.

Posted by Lora Petrova on April 6, 2016

How to deal with exam stress

We, here at The Student Guide, are sure the majority of our readers have experienced exam-related stress. We've all tried to escape it, but it sometimes feels impossible. Do not worry, however, as we have some advice for reducing stress during the exam period.


    Stay healthy

  • The key to staying healthy is to eat healthily; drink plenty of water and to be sure that you have minimum six hours sleep. A healthy body leads to a healthy state of mind.

    • Keep a balance

  • If you want to be successful in your exam preparation, you have to keep balance between your studies and your wellbeing. Remember, when you spend more that 40 minutes studying you have to take a rest for approximately twenty minutes. This way, you rest your brain and retain more information as you're not overloading yourself.
  • Don't forget your social life! When you are under pressure and are struggling to study, take a break and talk to your friends - chances are that they're experiencing stress over their exams too, you're not alone!

    • How to prepare for exam

  • Plan your schedule. Think of how much revision you have to do and how much time you need to spend on each subject to achieve your desired grade. Do not leave everything to the last minute!
  • Plan how much time you can spend on each question, how much time you have to read it and understand it.
  • Think of what you will be doing after the exam. How you'll spend your holidays, all of the friends you'll see!

The end of the world!


  • When the day comes, try not to be nervous. Focus on the paper.
  • Take a deep breath and try to not think about "What will happen if..."
  • If there are noises that distract you tell to your invigilator to do something.
  • Try to concentrate on the questions and your answers, rather than the "What ifs". Believe me, the stress will go away itself.

Wish you good luck with your upcoming exams! And remember, after every storm there is a rainbow!
