The Student Guide’s Ultimate Festival Guide

With fresh artists on the music scene, where should you spend your summer to hear all of the best acts?

Posted by Kelly Evans on April 6, 2016

The festival scene this year is filled with some fantastic acts from all genres, with old favourites and fresh artists with promising careers ahead of them. So what do we recommend for the summer festival tour?

Parklife

The Manchester weekender is presenting some of the best international acts and home-grown talent such as MOBO award winners Stormzy and Skepta.

When: 11 – 12 June 2016

Where: Heaton Park, Manchester

Headline Acts: Major Lazer and the Chemical Brothers


Isle of Wight Festival

Isle of Wight Festival is coming back with a bang and a host of great acts, reaching back to its roots presenting some of the top music talent. This year’s event is bound to be a good one with rock Legends Queen as one of the headline acts.

When: 9 – 12 June 2016

Where: Newport, Isle of Wight

Headline Acts: Queen, Faithless, The Stereophonics


V Festival

V Festival is turning 21 this year and it’s celebrating with all the top acts, including this year’s hugely successful headliners Rihanna and Justin Bieber. Look out for other top acts such as Sia, David Guetta, Bastille and Little Mix.

When: 20 – 21 August 2016

Where: Chelmsford / Weston-under-Lizard

Headline Acts: Justin Bieber, Rihanna


Reading and Leeds

For the more alternative taste, Reading and Leeds’ line-up of some of the most prominent indie, punk and metal artists will be the highlight of your musical summer.

When: 26 – 28 August 2016

Where: Reading, Leeds

Headline Acts: Red Hot Chilli Peppers, The 1975, Courteneers


6 Music Festival

Our wildcard this year is 6 Music Festival, presenting top music talent perhaps more on the fringe, so if you’re looking to expand your music taste or find some great up-and-coming bands, this is the festival to check out!

When: 12 – 14 February 2016

Where: Bristol

Headline Acts: Primal Scream, Foals, Suede
